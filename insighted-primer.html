InsightED as a centralized data collection platform

InsightED is a mobile-first ecosystem for collecting, validating, monitoring, and analyzing school, infrastructure, personnel, finance, SIIF, and support data. It connects school heads, engineers, division offices, regional offices, central office units, finance users, and support teams through role-specific portals and STRIDE-style decision dashboards.

🏫

School-Level Capture

  • Captures school profile, learners, classes, resources, facilities, location, and infrastructure needs.
  • Supports school heads working in low-connectivity field conditions.
🏗️

Infrastructure Monitoring

  • Tracks infrastructure projects from creation to completion.
  • Organizes inspections, photos, damage reports, donated projects, and progress updates.
📍

RO/SDO Oversight

  • Helps regional and division offices supervise schools under their jurisdiction.
  • Supports SIIF review, ESF7 validation, audits, user access, and location management.
🏛️

Central-Office Analytics

  • Shows national-level summaries and program dashboards for Central Office teams.
  • Connects class-size analysis, school head records, personnel registries, finance views, and facilities monitoring.
💸

SIIF and Finance Visibility

  • Connects school plan submissions to allocation, budget, appropriation, and utilization.
  • Helps RO and SDO users identify schools that need review or follow-up.
🛟

Support Ticketing

  • Gives users a structured way to report issues and request help.
  • Helps support teams review attachments, update ticket status, triage concerns, and track resolved cases.
Positioning: InsightED’s value is not only digitizing forms. Its value is turning verified field records into operational intelligence for school, division, regional, and national decision-making.

Salient Features

InsightED’s salient features are grouped into clear capability areas so users can distinguish between data capture, monitoring, data quality, communication, architecture, and offline-readiness benefits.

📥

Data Capture and Field Submission

Features that help schools submit and update information quickly from the field.

⚡

Real-Time Data Collection

Schools can update their records as new information becomes available, keeping school-level data current instead of waiting for delayed manual consolidation.

📶

Offline Mode

The PWA can store data in the device browser cache for offline functionality, helping users continue work even when connectivity is unstable.

👁️

Monitoring and Dashboard Visibility

Features that move submitted information into review and decision surfaces faster.

📡

Real-Time Monitoring

Regional and division offices can monitor school submissions and school data in real time, making follow-up faster and more targeted.

📊

Straight to Data Dashboards

Raw submissions flow directly into dashboards, helping users move from field input to management-level visibility without a separate reporting pipeline.

🧼

Data Quality and Validation

Features that keep records cleaner before they are used for analysis and reporting.

🧠

Analysis-Ready Data

Because records are stored in PostgreSQL with strict data type standards, submitted data needs little to no input cleaning before analysis.

✅

Internal Validation Logic

In-app data logic can check entries before submission, reducing misentry cases and helping users correct issues while they are still completing the form.

🔒

Input Restrictions

Fields can restrict entries by character type, number format, or character length, keeping data cleaner at the point of capture.

📣

Communication and User Guidance

Features that help administrators reach users directly inside the system.

📣

In-App Announcements

Administrators can post announcements, reminders, and alerts directly inside the app so important updates reach users in the same workspace where they submit data.

🧩

Platform Architecture and Maintainability

Features that make the platform cohesive for users while remaining flexible for the development team.

🧩

All-in-One App, Modular Design

Users experience InsightED as one cohesive app, while the system remains modular by design so teams can update one module without disrupting the others.

Modules Being Built

InsightED is organized into core module families that support data capture, validation, monitoring, analytics, and support operations across the platform.

🧭

Nexus and Role-Based Entry

🎯 PurposeThis is the front door of InsightED. It detects or lets users choose the correct portal so each role lands in the right dashboard, tools, and navigation flow.
💡 Why it mattersInsightED is not one screen for everyone. The Nexus layer prevents confusion by turning a multi-portal product into a single guided experience.
✅ Primary outcomeUsers reach the correct tools faster, while administrators can test or supervise multiple role contexts without creating separate accounts for every portal.
🏫

School Head Data Collection and Verification

🎯 PurposeThis is the field data-capture layer for school-level information. It replaces scattered reporting with modular school submissions that can be reviewed, synced, and reused in dashboards.
🗂️ Data capturedUnits 1–9 cover school identity, learners, organized classes, learner profile, shifting modality, school resources, physical facilities, school location, and infrastructure needs.
✅ Primary outcomeSchool heads can submit complete and traceable school records even with poor internet, while division and regional offices receive cleaner data for monitoring and planning.
🏗️

Infrastructure and Engineering Operations

🎯 PurposeThis layer tracks infrastructure projects from creation to monitoring, inspection, evidence upload, and validation. It gives engineers and implementers a shared project record.
🛠️ Field workflowsSite inspection, damage assessment, material inventory, school resources forms, progress updates, photo evidence, and implementation status tracking.
✅ Primary outcomeLeadership can see which projects are planned, ongoing, delayed, damaged, donated, or completed, while field teams can keep project evidence in one auditable place.
🏢

RO / SDO Monitoring and School Management

🎯 PurposeThis layer helps regional and division offices supervise schools under their jurisdiction. It turns school submissions and operational records into manageable lists, audits, and dashboards.
🔍 Review workflowsUsers can inspect school records, validate submissions, check personnel-related ESF7 data, manage account access, review location data, and follow up on schools that need action.
✅ Primary outcomeRO and SDO teams get a clearer picture of school compliance, data quality, jurisdiction coverage, and operational priorities without manually consolidating spreadsheets.
💸

SIIF Monitoring and Finance Visibility

🎯 PurposeThe SIIF layer connects school plan submissions with allocation, budget, appropriation, utilization, and review status. It is built for monitoring which schools or divisions need follow-up.
📈 Monitoring workflowsRO users can compare divisions, SDO users can inspect schools, and finance/program users can review budget-related figures and utilization status.
✅ Primary outcomeSIIF becomes easier to track from regional overview down to individual school details, with financial and submission signals visible in the same workflow.
🏛️

EFD / HRODI / Facilities and HR Monitoring

🎯 PurposeThis layer supports central facilities and personnel oversight. It connects infrastructure, construction, class-size, registry, and HR-related monitoring in one ecosystem.
📋 Program workflowsUsers can track facilities programs, review construction progress, inspect MOA-linked projects, analyze class-size pressure, and maintain official or school-head registry data.
✅ Primary outcomeCentral teams can see how facilities, staffing, class-size, and program records relate to each other instead of reviewing each stream in isolation.
📊

HQ and Central-Office Analytics

🎯 PurposeThe HQ layer gives national users a controlled workspace for high-level monitoring, registry lookup, school search, finance review, personnel analytics, and facilities analytics.
🧠 Decision workflowsHQ users can inspect school records, compare national indicators, review personnel and facilities data, and connect finance or program signals to operational evidence.
✅ Primary outcomeNational leadership receives decision-ready views instead of raw field submissions, helping translate verified data into policy, allocation, and program actions.
🛟

Ticketing, QA, and Support Operations

🎯 PurposeThis layer gives InsightED a structured support channel. Users can report problems, attach evidence, follow ticket status, and receive updates while support teams triage and resolve concerns.
🤝 Support workflowsSupport staff can review issues, manage attachments, update statuses, document resolutions, publish announcements, and identify recurring rollout problems.
✅ Primary outcomeField issues become trackable operational data, giving the rollout team a clearer way to stabilize the platform and respond to users.

How InsightED connects to STRIDE

InsightED is the capture, workflow, validation, and monitoring layer. STRIDE is the strategic analytics and decision-support layer. Together, they turn verified operational records into resource planning, infrastructure monitoring, and leadership dashboards.

📥
Capture InsightED collects school, project, budget, HR, SIIF, and support records directly from field and office users.
✅
Validate School heads, engineers, RO/SDO teams, and HQ users review submissions so dashboards use trusted records.
🗄️
Persist Validated information is saved as database records, evidence files, activity logs, and audit-ready histories.
📊
Analyze InsightED turns verified data into dashboards, maps, metrics, summaries, and program-level monitoring signals.
🎯
Decide Leaders use those signals to guide allocation, follow-up, reporting, policy, and operational action.

Core Identifiers

 Defines record uniqueness and synchronization metadata.

  • School name
  • Region, division, and district
  • Map location coordinates

 Stores school location, leadership, curricular, ownership, and establishment profile.
Unit 2: Learners
  • Total number of learners
  • Male learner count
  • Female learner count

 Captures enrollment by grade level, gender, and SNED counts.
Unit 3: Organized Classes
  • Whether the school has multigrade classes
  • Number of multigrade sections
  • Class and section counts by grade level
 Tracks section counts, class sizes, and multigrade operations.
Unit 4: Learner Profile
  • Learner groups served by the school
  • Nutrition and health status counts
  • Alternative Learning System learner counts
  • Indigenous Peoples learner count

 Captures vulnerable groups, health indicators, ALS, IP, and ALIVE program counts.

  • Whether the school uses shifting schedules
